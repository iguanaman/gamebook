# Geometry Dash: The Takeover — Story Brief

## Setting
Present-day. The reader is a 10-year-old kid bussed into the secret HQ of RobTop Games. Part factory, part theme park, part death-trap obstacle course — Charlie-and-the-Chocolate-Factory with neon cubes. Spike-forging halls. Portal labs. Wave tunnels. Jump-pad lines.

Tone: bright, hyperactive, faintly menacing. Wonder up front, attrition underneath. Other kids get eliminated one per room as they fail RobTop's quiz. Spectacle, not gore.

## Hook
RobTop is retiring and has invited six superfan kids to tour HQ. Whoever proves they know Geometry Dash best inherits the studio. Wrong answer = out.

## Player Role
You: a 10-year-old GD obsessive, one of the finalists. Six kids in the foyer, one of you walks out with the keys. Standing in your way: deep-cut GD lore questions and the HQ's "elimination mechanisms."

## Arc Shape
Linear gauntlet, branching at every quiz gate. Five rooms. Each room poses one deep-cut lore question — no easy ramp, all questions hard. Right answer → next room, with one fewer rival. Wrong answer → themed elimination scene, dead end. Final room is a 1v1 with Dex.

## Endings
Two outcomes:
- **Win** — survive all five quiz gates, named RobTop's successor.
- **Eliminated** — one bad-ending scene per room, themed to the elimination mechanism.

## Constraints
~12 scenes total: foyer + 5 quiz rooms + 5 elimination scenes + 1 win scene.
Stats: **none.** Flags: **none.** No `visited`, no conditional text blocks, no hubs, no random events.

**Prose density:** every scene is short — get to the question fast, minimal environmental description, minimal interior monologue. Each quiz scene fits on a phone screen. The narrator is the player's interior voice (10-year-old, fragmenty), but tight.

**Choice density:** each quiz scene has 4 choices — 1 correct + 3 plausible-sounding wrongs (all routing to the same elimination scene). Correct answer placement varies — never always first.

This deliberately ignores hubs/random/multiple-paths from the standard checklist — the story is a quiz, not a sandbox.

## Tone Reference
Charlie and the Chocolate Factory crossed with Geometry Dash's neon-on-black visual language. Kid-friendly menace.

## Visual Feel
Near-black background with bright neon accents — cyan primary, magenta secondary, green/yellow supporting. Sharp geometric edges, zero rounding, arcade/EDM display fonts.
