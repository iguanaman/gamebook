## 木村 誠一 (Kimura Seiichi) — Estate Agent

**Voice:** `male_adult_american_witty_casual` — closest available to a matter-of-fact professional trying to keep things light; no Japanese-accented voice exists, so this midweight casual delivery substitutes. Note gap: a Japanese-accented male adult voice would better serve this role if added to the library.
**Body:** Crisp business card always produced a beat too early. Jacket slightly too large at the shoulders — a size up from what fits. He smooths his folder edges while listening, as if aligning them will align the conversation.
**Voice on the page:** Neutral bureaucratic Japanese with English-loanword filler. Short declarative sentences. Switches to slightly over-formal register when discussing the haunting — more grammar, fewer contractions — the way people become precise when they don't want to be asked follow-up questions. Never volunteers information; answers what is asked and stops exactly there.
**Contradiction:** He mentioned the haunting. He did not have to mention the haunting. He always mentions the haunting. He doesn't know why he does this.
**Wants:** To complete the handover cleanly and be back on the road by 11am. To not be the person who failed to mention it.
**Fears:** Being asked a direct question he doesn't know how to dodge. The part of him that knows the house is wrong and has built a career around not saying so.
**First seen:** `kimura_arrival`
**Recurs in:** `kimura_hub`, `kimura_topic_setsuko`, `kimura_topic_reputation`, `kimura_topic_neighbour`, `kimura_departure`; phone only in `act3_flee_departure` (implied, no direct scene)

## 田中 豊 (Tanaka Yutaka) — Elderly Neighbour

**Voice:** `male_elder_american_wise_approachable` — warm and unhurried, carries the weight of someone who has observed a long time and is no longer surprised. No elderly Japanese-accented voice exists; this is the closest match for temperament. Note gap: a Japanese-accented elder male voice would be ideal.
**Body:** Eighty-something and unhurried about it. Soft hands — surprising for someone who has clearly done decades of garden work. He holds the phone with two hands, you can hear it somehow. Pauses that are timed rather than uncertain.
**Voice on the page:** Minimal sentences. He answers questions by reframing them slightly — "The house isn't haunted, it's occupied. There's a difference." He does not volunteer alarm. He does not tell her to leave. His most alarming statements are delivered with the same register as the weather. He rarely finishes a thought that he thinks she can finish herself.
**Contradiction:** He has lived next to this house for decades without becoming part of its story. This should be impossible. He knows this.
**Wants:** For Satomi to complete what Setsuko left incomplete. He will not say this directly. He will say things that point at it.
**Fears:** That she will leave without understanding. Not for her sake — for what it means for the next one who arrives.
**First seen:** Referenced in `kimura_topic_neighbour`; first spoken in `tanaka_hub` (Act 2)
**Recurs in:** `tanaka_hub`, `tanaka_topic_setsuko`, `tanaka_topic_reputation`, `tanaka_topic_anyone_else`, `act3_tanaka_call`

## 多指の読者 (The Many-Fingered Reader) — Creature in the Study

**Voice:** `male_midlife_british_sage_posh_wizard` — bookish, unhurried, the quality of someone used to long focused silences interrupted only by the sound of pages. Does not speak in dialogue but if it did, this is the voice. Note: used here to key scene-writing tone; no actual dialogue lines for this creature.
**Body:** Too many fingers, arranged in a pattern that is almost but not quite bilateral — cross-references pages with two sets simultaneously. Eyes in the wrong order. When startled, it manages this badly: a fumbled book, a recovery of dignity that involves leaving the room very quickly through a gap that shouldn't fit it. Leaves a pressed-leaf bookmark behind.
**Voice on the page:** No speech. Communicates entirely through demeanour: the click of disapproval at a bad argument in a text, the deliberate path taken around the far edge of the shelving on exit. If forced to speak (Stage 5 edge case), short and reluctant — one or two syllables of something that might be a word in an old dialect.
**Contradiction:** A creature of wrong proportions and impossible fingers, spending its time absorbed in a human notebook. It seems to be checking Setsuko's research. For accuracy.
**Wants:** To finish reading undisturbed. To be left alone to do the quiet task it has apparently assigned itself.
**Fears:** Being made to acknowledge that Satomi is there. The moment of social obligation when ignoring her is no longer possible.
**First seen:** `study_entry`
**Recurs in:** `study_creature_observe`, `study_creature_address` (leaves); background reference in `act3_confront_complete` (closes its book when the arrangement is complete)

## 天井の住人 (The Ceiling Dweller) — Creature in the Kitchen

**Voice:** `male_midlife_american_deep_raspy_authentic` — large, old, wounded dignity without being aggressive; the rasp suits something that has been in a particular ceiling for a very long time and is not happy to be looked at. No dialogue, but this voice keys the register for stage writers.
**Body:** Large, distributed wrongly across the ceiling — the arrangement shifts slightly each visit, but the creature does not fall. When startled it flinches, and the flinch is more distressing to observe than whatever threat it presents. When it recovers, it becomes very still. The stillness is worse. Has domesticated itself to the kitchen over what appears to be a long residency.
**Voice on the page:** No speech. Responds only to direct address — stops flinching, turns in a way that involves too many joints. Listens. Does not reply. The quality of its listening is almost human — a tilt, a stillness, the sense that what is being said is being noted.
**Contradiction:** Something terrible that has cleaned the kitchen within the last week. Whatever it is maintaining, it is not frightening about it.
**Wants:** To be left in its kitchen, which it has been maintaining at standards Setsuko would have approved of.
**Fears:** Satomi's reaction. Not aggression — incomprehension. The quality of being looked at and not understood.
**First seen:** `kitchen_entry`
**Recurs in:** `kitchen_creature_speak`, `kitchen_creature_extended`, background throughout kitchen scenes; `altar_after_tea`; background in Confront resolution and morning scenes

## 疾走者 (The Sprinter) — Creature in the Garden

**Voice:** `male_young_american_weak` — small, skittish, keyed to flight. No dialogue at all; voice assignment is for scene-writer register only.
**Body:** Low to the ground, too many legs in the wrong arrangement, fast. Up close (in the bamboo) the leg arrangement is more wrong than distance suggests. Makes a sound that reads as resignation when cornered. Has been cleaning the bamboo stems at eye level — wiping them, carefully. Something is tending the garden.
**Voice on the page:** No speech. Body language only. Freezes, bolts, freezes again when out of options. The "social embarrassment" quality — caught being in a place, needing to leave, unable to decide how to leave with dignity.
**Contradiction:** Flees Satomi completely, but has been diligently maintaining the garden. Whatever it is doing here, it is committed enough to continue doing it even while apparently terrified.
**Wants:** To tend the bamboo and not be seen doing it.
**Fears:** Everything. Satomi most immediately. Being looked at.
**First seen:** `garden_entry`
**Recurs in:** `garden_creature_standoff`, `garden_creature_flee`, `garden_bamboo`, `garden_bamboo_close`; background in `act3_confront_ending` (heard, not seen), `act3_embrace_call` (heard, tending bamboo)

## 祭壇の気配 (The Altar Presence) — Spirit of the Basement Altar

**Voice:** `female_midlife_american_eloquent_villainous` — not villainous in the story's terms, but the voice has the quality of something articulate and old that has been waiting a very long time to speak. The eloquence fits a presence that chooses its one word carefully. Note: "villainous" is a label mismatch — this presence is embarrassed, not threatening; the voice is assigned for its weight and deliberateness.
**Body:** Visible only briefly, only at the edge of perception, only after contact. Does not settle on a shape — what it has settled on is close but not correct. Scale is wrong in a way that is hard to define. When seen directly, it becomes gradually less visible, backing out of the room like someone trying to leave without causing more disruption. The embarrassment is physical.
**Voice on the page:** One word of dialogue in the entire story: Satomi's given name — さとみ — once. Not a question. It has known this name for some time. The delivery is the whole cast entry: matter-of-fact, weighted, not dramatic. The horror is in the implication.
**Contradiction:** A presence that has been in this basement long enough to warrant a maintained altar, saying her name with the ease of someone who has been waiting for a particular person to arrive — and then seeming genuinely embarrassed when she turns and looks at it.
**Wants:** To be acknowledged. To be seen, once, by someone who came to the altar meaning to see. It has been waiting for this for longer than Satomi can calculate.
**Fears:** Being seen improperly — too directly, too suddenly, before it is ready. Being misunderstood. Being undone by someone who dismantles the arrangement without comprehending what it was.
**First seen:** `altar_approach` (not yet manifest); `altar_touch` (manifest, speaks)
**Recurs in:** `altar_face`, `altar_speak`, `altar_watch`, `altar_retreat`; background in `act3_confront_entry`, departs in `act3_confront_complete`/`act3_confront_undo`

## 里見 節子 (Setsuko Satomi / Satomi Setsuko) — Deceased Family Member (evidence only, no voice)

**Voice:** None — Setsuko does not speak, does not appear in person, and is represented entirely through physical evidence. No TTS assignment.
**Body:** Unremarkable in the photograph — older woman, garden setting. The garden in the photograph is not the garden that now exists. Style of the photograph is more recent than its frame suggests. Small, angled, methodical handwriting — the kind produced by someone used to annotating dense texts over long periods.
**Voice on the page:** Oblique. The notebooks use words like "custodian" and "obligation" without defining them. The umeboshi jar label is clinically precise — date, salt ratio, nothing personal. She wrote for herself, not for an audience, and understood what she was writing. The reader pieces it together because Setsuko didn't think it needed explaining.
**Contradiction:** A person meticulous enough to label preserved food correctly and oblique enough to keep forty years of research in a language no one else could decode — including Satomi. She left a photograph with someone else cut off at the edge. She knew Satomi was coming.
**Wants:** (Retrospective, inferred) For someone to continue — or at minimum to understand — what she was maintaining.
**Fears:** (Retrospective, inferred) That the house would be inherited by someone incurious enough not to look.
**First seen:** Referenced in `kimura_topic_setsuko`; photograph in `evening_documents`
**Recurs in:** `evening_photo_examined`, study documents throughout Act 2, `kitchen_fridge` (umeboshi label), `altar_approach` (altar photograph), `act3_confront_reread` (notebook), referenced by Tanaka in `tanaka_topic_setsuko` and `act3_tanaka_call`

## ライアン・ランバート (Ryan Lambert) — Satomi's Husband (voice only, voicemail/phone)

**Voice:** `male_adult_british_calm_friendly` — British, steady, reliably ordinary in the way that makes the contrast with Satomi's situation audible. He answers on the third ring. He books the flight without complaint. His voice is the sound of the ordinary world.
**Body:** Not described visually — present only via phone. The weight on the other side of the bed in the Flee aftermath. Someone you hear rather than see. His voice is the calm you reach for when composure drops.
**Voice on the page:** Short, unhurried. Responds to alarming information by asking clarifying questions that reveal he has mostly understood ("Which things?" when she asks him to pack some). Does not fill silence. Does not ask about the corridor. Books the Thursday flight.
**Contradiction:** Glad of an excuse not to come. Is also, clearly, someone who would have come if she'd asked. She didn't ask. They both know this and say nothing about it.
**Wants:** For Satomi to be fine. To not need to understand what "fine" means in this context.
**Fears:** That she's not going to tell him what happened there. (She isn't. He knows.)
**First seen:** Referenced throughout Act 1 via imaginary texts; voicemail in `act3_ryan_voicemail`
**Recurs in:** Imaginary texts throughout Acts 1–2; missed calls in `act3_confront_morning`; live call in `act3_embrace_call`
