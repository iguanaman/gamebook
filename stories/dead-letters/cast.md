# Dead Letters — Cast

## Vera Doss (the client)
**Voice:** `female_adult_american_gentle_tender_sweet` — a tender, careful register fits a frightened young widow holding back what she knows; the sweetness reads as the shell over her fear, not as innocence.
**Body:** thin gold wedding band she keeps turning on her finger; a small dark stain on the cuff of her good coat she hasn't noticed.
**Voice on the page:** Short sentences. Polite to a fault — calls Frank "Mr. Cady" until the second meeting. Avoids the word *dead*; says "if he doesn't come back" and "if anything's happened." Apologises for taking up time. When she's most afraid she gets quieter, not louder.
**Contradiction:** Asks Frank to find her husband, then flinches every time Frank gets close to finding him. She wants Ray back and she wants Frank to fail; she can't say which more.
**Wants:** to be told Ray is alive; failing that, to know whatever Ray died for was worth the silence she's been carrying.
**Fears:** that Ray told her too much already, and that the men he was afraid of know it.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 1 (Frank's office — `vera_intro`).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 Beat 5 (`vera_room` and follow-ons); body discovered in Act 1 Beat 6 (`vera_room_dead` / `vera_room_dead_sneak`). Referenced posthumously throughout Acts 2–3 (the empty chair, the photograph in Frank's wallet).

## Mort Halloran (press contact)
**Voice:** `male_elder_american_wise_approachable` — older newsroom hand; warmth and worn humour fit a man who has watched the city eat itself for thirty years and still likes the work.
**Body:** rumpled brown suit dusted with cigarette ash; thumb permanently inked from the desk pen.
**Voice on the page:** Talks in headlines. Tells stories sideways. Drops a fact mid-joke and watches to see if Frank caught it. Calls everyone "kid" including men older than him.
**Contradiction:** Plays the harmless old hack but knows exactly which alderman is sleeping with which clerk; the gossip is the cover, not the content.
**Wants:** one more page-one byline before they retire him to obituaries.
**Fears:** that the *Examiner* is already part of what he'd need to expose, and he'd be writing into a drawer.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 2 (`mort_hub` — Frank's office, optional).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 Beat 6 (`mort_aftermath`); Act 2 Beat 2 (`mort_visit`), Beat 6 (`squeeze_mort` and variants — including `squeeze_mort_dora` for the address handoff); Act 3 Beat 1 (`mort_phone_call`, `mort_drop_package`), Beat 3 (`mort_basement`), phone presence in `morning_room`.

## Cyril Voss (Ray's supervisor)
**Voice:** `male_adult_british_clear_posh_heartfelt` — posh, professional, faintly heartfelt — fits a career bureaucrat who genuinely thinks of himself as a decent man while his department launders land titles.
**Body:** half-moon reading spectacles he polishes when he's lying; a small enamel pin on his lapel from a charity dinner he hosted.
**Voice on the page:** Civil-service English, mildly transatlantic. "I'm afraid that file is in transit, Mr. Cady." Uses *one* instead of *I* when he's most uncomfortable. Never quite says no.
**Contradiction:** Will give Frank tea and stonewall him in the same sentence. Genuinely liked Ray Doss and is genuinely afraid of what Ray was about to do.
**Wants:** retirement in eighteen months, intact pension, a quiet house.
**Fears:** that Ray's notes are still inside the building and that whoever asks for them next will not be Frank.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 3 (`voss_office`).
**Recurs in:** Referenced only in Act 2 Beat 4 (`records_voss_note`) — his handwriting on the note that names Walt Kellner; no spoken Act 2 line.

## Edmund Tice (junior clerk, City Engineer's Office)
**Voice:** `male_young_american_weak` — young, thin, anxious; fits a clerk too frightened to say what he knows out loud. Distinct from Voss's heartfelt posh and from any other young American voice in the cast.
**Body:** ink-stained shirt cuff he keeps tucking; a wristwatch a size too large that slides on his forearm.
**Voice on the page:** Half-finished sentences. Says the noun and lets Frank fill in the verb. "Ray was — yeah." Looks at the corridor before he speaks. Calls Frank "sir" without thinking.
**Contradiction:** Wants to be the one who told someone, and is going to spend the next ten years pretending he wasn't.
**Wants:** to keep his job and to have done the right thing — in that order, and he knows it.
**Fears:** Voss noticed; Voss didn't notice and the next person to ask isn't Frank.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 3 (`engineers_corridor` — the stairwell after Voss's office).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 only — single-scene cameo, with three follow-on framings (`tice_name`, `tice_spills`, `tice_bolts`). Referenced by name in Act 2 if Frank revisits the engineer's office.

## Lou Mancuso (Iron Lung bartender)
**Voice:** `male_adult_american_witty_casual` — relaxed, wry, working bar; fits a man who has heard everything twice and is paid to forget it the third time.
**Body:** towel always over one shoulder; missing the top of the left ring finger from a job before this one.
**Voice on the page:** Half-sentences. Answers the question after the one he was asked. "Ray. Yeah. Thursday. Quiet, mostly." Calls Frank "buddy" while clearly remembering exactly who he is.
**Contradiction:** Pretends not to remember the regulars and remembers everything; pretends he doesn't take sides and quietly does.
**Wants:** to keep his bar out of any newspaper anyone reads.
**Fears:** the eavesdropper at the end of the bar coming back with friends.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 4 (`iron_lung_lou` and follow-ons).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 only. Not referenced in later acts on the canonical paths.

## Mrs. Pell (boarding house landlady)
**Voice:** `female_elder_american_relatable_likeable` — elder American, warm in ordinary weather and shaken when the weather changes; fits a woman who has run rooms for thirty years and has never had a tenant die in one.
**Body:** apron pockets that always have a folded letter or a bobby pin in them; a chain of small keys at her hip she touches when she's flustered.
**Voice on the page:** Run-on sentences when alarmed; clipped when she's lying. Calls Frank "the gentleman" to other people and "Mr. Cady" to his face. Apologises for the stairs. Pretends not to have noticed things she has counted twice.
**Contradiction:** Likes Vera and was paid this morning by a man in a good suit not to mention the second visitor; will tell Frank anyway if he asks the right way.
**Wants:** the house to stay quiet and the rent to keep coming.
**Fears:** the man in the good suit coming back.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 5 (`vera_visit_intro`).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 Beat 6 (`boarding_house_summons`, `boarding_house_arrive`, `pell_aside`). Not referenced after Act 1.
**Voice-share note:** shares `female_elder_american_relatable_likeable` with Hettie Voorhees (Act 2 Beat 8). The two never share a scene and are an act apart; per principles, single-act elder cameos may double up. Acceptable.

## Detective Sergeant Pat Brennan (homicide cop)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_american_dark_tough` — dark, hard-edged American cop voice; fits a man who runs a scene by volume and presence.
**Body:** loosened tie, sweat-darkened collar; a knuckle on his right hand permanently swollen.
**Voice on the page:** Imperatives. "Off the stoop, Cady." Doesn't ask questions he doesn't already know the answer to. Uses Frank's last name like a slur. Speaks in short bursts when angry, longer sentences when he's lying.
**Contradiction:** Honestly believes he is one of the good ones, which is why he is so useful to the bad ones.
**Wants:** the Doss death closed by Friday and his name nowhere on the paperwork.
**Fears:** that one day he'll be told to do something he can't talk himself out of.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 6 (`boarding_house_arrive` and follow-ons).
**Recurs in:** Act 2 Beat 6 (`squeeze_brennan`); Act 3 Beat 4 (`morning_room`) — voice on the radio reading the wire copy that names Frank as "person of interest." No on-page Act 3 scene (a deliberate narrative wound — Brennan speaks but never appears).

## Detective Costa (uniform tail)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_american_loud_confident_cocky` — loud, confident, cocky; distinct from Brennan's hard bark and Kellner's smooth warmth. The cop sent to lean on Frank's stairwell when Brennan won't come himself.
**Body:** a hat brim sweat-stained at the band; a toothpick he chews through three a shift.
**Voice on the page:** Joke first, threat second, both meant. Calls Frank "Frankie" because he knows Frank hates it. Talks past Frank to anyone listening on the stair — performance for an audience that isn't there.
**Contradiction:** Wants Frank to like him while delivering the warning that ends the friendship before it starts.
**Wants:** Brennan's job in five years; a transfer out of the unit if Brennan gets promoted out instead.
**Fears:** that the man who ordered him here will remember he was here.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 1 (`stairwell_warning` — Exit B/D high-heat entry only).
**Recurs in:** Act 2 only — single-scene cameo. Referenced once by Brennan in Beat 6 dialogue if the player triggered him.

## Marta Gaines (Aldwick organiser)
**Voice:** `female_midlife_african-american_casual_opinionated` — midlife, working-class, opinionated; the woman who runs the meeting because nobody else has the breath for it. Casual register undercuts the gravity of what she's saying, which is the point.
**Body:** ledger of her own — a school exercise book with names of evicted neighbours and dates ruled in pencil; a chipped tooth she shows when she laughs without meaning to.
**Voice on the page:** Plain talk. Names things. "They served Reuben on a Tuesday and the bulldozer was there Friday." Asks Frank questions and waits for the whole answer before saying anything. Doesn't perform her grief; will tell a joke about it instead and let Frank decide whether to laugh.
**Contradiction:** Distrusts every white man with a notebook and is the reason this case can be solved. Talks to Frank because the alternative is that Ray Doss died for nothing — not because she's decided he's worth talking to.
**Wants:** the rezoning order on her brother's house reversed and the highway turned a half-mile north. Will not get either, and knows it.
**Fears:** that her name in Frank's notebook is the thing that gets her brother killed before the bulldozer arrives.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 3 (`church_hall_meeting`).
**Recurs in:** Act 2 Beat 3 across kitchen and topic scenes (`marta_kitchen`, `marta_full_account`, `marta_partial`, `marta_key_thread`, `marta_reuben`, `marta_apology`); absent on the third Aldwick visit (`reuben_porch` — Marta is in Detroit). Act 3 Beat 4 (`aldwick_farewell`, optional).

## Pastor Lemuel Briggs (Aldwick clergy)
**Voice:** `male_adult_american_casual_relaxed_light` — relaxed, light American register; a working pastor who jokes more than he sermonises and uses the joke to land the harder truth. Distinct from Mort's elder warmth and from Lou's wry barkeep.
**Body:** clerical collar always slightly crooked; a small leather notebook of phone numbers in his breast pocket — half the names crossed out.
**Voice on the page:** Easy cadence. Quotes scripture rarely and badly when he does. Asks how Frank is twice in a row. Shifts from joke to flat statement without changing tone. Says "the Lord" the way most men say "the city" — as something that is going to do what it's going to do.
**Contradiction:** Has been a pastor for twenty years and has stopped believing the prayer changes anything; still says it because somebody in the room needs to hear it. Trusts Marta's judgement of people more than his own.
**Wants:** the church hall to keep being a church hall when the bulldozers come.
**Fears:** that the next funeral he runs is one of his congregation, and that he will not have anything to say at it.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 3 (`church_hall_meeting`, low-heat path).
**Recurs in:** Aldwick first-visit follow-ons (`marta_intro_*`, `marta_intro_listen`); absent on the third visit (a narrative wound — no on-screen explanation). Referenced in Act 3 Beat 4 by Marta in `aldwick_farewell` (a town two states over, a sister) — no on-screen Act 3 line.

## Reuben Gaines (Marta's brother)
**Voice:** `male_adult_african-american_uneducated` — Aldwick working man, plain register; the brother whose house is the test case for the whole rezoning scheme. Distinct from Marta's casual opinionated cadence — Reuben is quieter, slower, more tired than his sister.
**Body:** a carpenter's hands gone stiff at the knuckles; a folded condemnation notice in his back pocket he takes out and refolds when he's thinking.
**Voice on the page:** Few words. Long pauses. Answers Frank's questions with another question and then answers his own. Calls Marta "my sister" rather than her name when other people are in the room. Says "the city" the way other people say "the weather" — as a thing that arrives.
**Contradiction:** Marta is the one who reads the documents to him and the one he protects from what they say.
**Wants:** to keep his porch.
**Fears:** that Marta's notebook is the reason their house is on the list.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 3 (`marta_intro_listen` — silent in the meeting; first speaks in `marta_kitchen` topic scenes, particularly `marta_reuben`, `reuben_keeps`, `reuben_gives`).
**Recurs in:** Act 2 Aldwick revisit (`reuben_porch`, `reuben_speaks`, `reuben_silent`); Act 3 Beat 4 possible off-page presence in `aldwick_farewell` (the next room, or a porch with the door open by Act 2 fallout).

## Floor janitor (City Engineer's after-hours)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_british_tough_calloused` — distinct from Voss's heartfelt posh and Salzano's seductive deep. A working caretaker with a tired bark; the only other Brit in the building besides those two and not mistakable for either.
**Body:** ring of keys at the belt heavier than it needs to be; rolled sleeves; a folded racing form in his back pocket.
**Voice on the page:** Tired imperatives. Tells Frank what to do without asking who he is. "Mind the wax." Doesn't ask for a name. Files the face for later if it costs him nothing.
**Contradiction:** Will let Frank in for a folded note, knowing exactly which floor Frank wants and exactly which file he's going to take, and will not tell anyone — until the day he is asked the right way, and then he will tell them everything.
**Wants:** the night shift over by four; the Saturday races.
**Fears:** Voss noticing the wax was scuffed where it shouldn't be.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 4 (`records_janitor` and `records_press_badge` entry routes).
**Recurs in:** Act 2 only — brief reappearance on the corridor exit (`records_corridor_exit`), where he does not look up from the racing form. Single-scene cameo otherwise.

## Salzano (real-estate fixer)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_british_dark_seductive_deep` — dark, deep, controlled; the polite voice a man uses when violence is already in the price. A faint English polish on a New York fixer reads as money, not foreignness. Distinct from Brennan's hot American bark and Kellner's smooth American warmth — Salzano is the one who never raises his.
**Body:** clean fingernails kept shorter than fashion; a small white scar through his upper lip he touches when he's amused.
**Voice on the page:** Quiet declaratives. Uses the conditional to threaten ("it would be a shame"). Calls Frank "Mr. Cady" with the same courtesy he'd call him by his first name; the difference between is unsettling. Never repeats himself. Does not need to.
**Contradiction:** Speaks like a lawyer, dresses like a banker, is the man the conspiracy sends when it wants someone hurt without paperwork. Genuinely believes he is the reasonable one in the room.
**Wants:** the Aldwick parcels cleared on schedule and Frank's name off the file by the end of the month — preferably with Frank still drawing breath, but not strictly.
**Fears:** that the men he works for will decide he is a liability the same way they decided Ray Doss was.
**First seen:** Silent presence in Act 1 Beat 4 (`iron_lung_door` / `iron_lung_watch` / `iron_lung_eavesdropper` — "the eavesdropper", no spoken line) and off-page in Act 1 Beats 5–6 (`pell_describes`, `pell_aside`). First speaks in Act 2 Beat 4 (`records_listening` off-page on phone; on-page in `records_corridor_intercept` and `salzano_offer`).
**Recurs in:** Act 2 Beat 6 (`squeeze_salzano`); Act 3 Beat 2 (`warehouse_reveal`, `warehouse_fight`, `warehouse_talk`, `warehouse_run`, `pavement_check`, `martyr_trigger`). His silent second man tracks alongside but is never casted (no spoken line).

## Walt Kellner (old partner / lieutenant, Organized Crime Unit)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_american_rich_smooth_rugged` — rich, smooth, rugged; the warmth that made Frank trust him for ten years and the polish that hides what the warmth is for. Distinct from Brennan's hard bark and from the narrator's worn drawl.
**Body:** wedding ring he no longer wears but the pale band on his finger gives it away; a fountain pen clipped to his jacket pocket that he uses to point with rather than write.
**Voice on the page:** Calls Frank "Cady" the way an older brother does. Long sentences when he's being honest, short ones when he's lying — the opposite of Brennan. Tells one true story to land three small lies. Asks after Frank's health, his rent, his dog (Frank doesn't have a dog) — uses warmth as a search.
**Contradiction:** Loves Frank the way men love the people they have already decided to sell. Means every kind thing he says and is going to do the unkind thing anyway because he believes the city will not let him do otherwise.
**Wants:** to keep his job, his pension, and Frank alive — in that order. Will cut the third loose if the first two require it.
**Fears:** that Frank already knows it was him who pushed him out of the force in '39, and that Frank is testing him every time he smiles.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 7 — phone in `kellner_phone`, then on-page in `kellner_diner` or `kellner_office`. Named earlier in Act 2 (`records_voss_note`, `kellner_in_ledger`) before he speaks.
**Recurs in:** Act 2 Beat 7 hub (`kellner_hub` and topic scenes); phone in Act 2 Beat 9 (`hallway_call`); Act 3 Beat 1 (`drive_river_arrive`), Beat 2 (`warehouse_doorway`, `kellner_talk_hub`, `kellner_topic_ray`, `kellner_topic_past`, `kellner_topic_offer`, `warehouse_offer_taken`, `warehouse_reveal`, `warehouse_fight`, `warehouse_talk`, `pavement_check`). Off-page after Beat 2; named in codas only.

## Hettie Voorhees (post office clerk)
**Voice:** `female_elder_american_relatable_likeable` — elder American, warm; a counter clerk who has weighed parcels for forty years.
**Body:** spectacles on a beaded chain; a rubber thumb-stall she only sometimes remembers to take off before handing back change.
**Voice on the page:** Brisk. Friendly without warmth. Calls every customer "dear" without looking up. Reads the weight off the scale aloud. Asks Frank twice if he wants the parcel insured because she is paid to ask twice.
**Contradiction:** Has been told by a man in a good suit to mention any package addressed to the *Tribune-Ledger* and has not yet decided whether she will.
**Wants:** her shift to end at six.
**Fears:** the man in the good suit coming back to check.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 8 (`package_post_office` and follow-ons — `package_no_address`, `package_mailed`, `package_mailed_insured`, `package_mailed_witness`, `package_aborted`).
**Recurs in:** Act 2 only — single-act cameo. The narrative wound of whether she made the call is left ambiguous on the witness path.
**Voice-share note:** shares `female_elder_american_relatable_likeable` with Mrs. Pell (Act 1). The two never share a scene and are an act apart; per principles, single-act elder cameos may double up. Acceptable.

## Dora Reyes (journalist)
**Voice:** `female_adult_american_crisp_direct_clear` — crisp, direct, clear; a working reporter who edits her own sentences mid-thought. Cuts cleanly across the older men in this story — Mort's warmth, Kellner's smoothness, Frank's drawl. Distinct from Vera's gentleness.
**Body:** ink on the side of her left hand from the spiral binding of the notebook she carries; a wristwatch she checks the way other people check a window.
**Voice on the page:** Short questions, short answers. Numbers things — "two reasons" — and then gives both. Doesn't soften refusals. The kindest thing she says to Frank is "yes, I'll print it if it's true."
**Contradiction:** Younger than Mort by twenty years and the only person in the press she'd trust with this; thinks of herself as cynical and has not yet been disappointed enough to be it.
**Wants:** a story she can run unedited. Has been at the smaller paper three years waiting for one.
**Fears:** that she will read Frank's package and not be brave enough to file it; or that she will be, and Frank will be dead before the second draft.
**First seen:** Referenced off-page through Act 2 (Mort, the package). First on-page voice in Act 3 Beat 4 (`dora_newsroom`, `dora_newsroom_commit`).
**Recurs in:** Act 3 Beat 5 (`final_choice_phone`); voiceover in Act 3 Beat 6 (`ending_martyr` — reads the lede of her Monday column). Present in person on the Expose path (`ending_expose` shading); posthumous voice on Martyr.

## Officer Daniel Quint (clean cop)
**Voice:** `male_young_american_forthright` — young, plain American, forthright; a uniform who hasn't yet learned to hedge. Distinct from Brennan's hard bark, Salzano's quiet danger, Kellner's smooth warmth — Quint is the only honest cop voice in the story and sounds it.
**Body:** uniform a half-size too large at the shoulders; a paperback in his patrol-car door pocket — he reads on shift; a wedding band still bright from the jeweller's box.
**Voice on the page:** Direct sentences. Says what he means and looks Frank in the face when he says it. No slang for the trade — never says "perp" or "collar"; says "the man" and "the arrest." Calls Frank "Mr. Cady" without irony. Has a habit of repeating Frank's own last sentence back to him before answering — a careful man working out whether what he just heard is true.
**Contradiction:** Came onto the force a year ago because he believed the work was clean and is realising, in real time, that it is not — and has decided to do the clean work anyway, alone, until somebody stops him. Will be told to stop within six months of the story closing.
**Wants:** to be the cop he thought he'd be when he took the oath; failing that, to be useful to one good case before they take it from him.
**Fears:** that Brennan will find out before Quint gets the chance to mean it.
**First seen:** Act 3, Beat 3 (`quint_patrol_car` — pulls his patrol car alongside Frank on the river bank).
**Recurs in:** Referenced in `morning_room` (Beat 4) if Quint helped (Frank wears Quint's shirt). Coda shading in `ending_expose` if `quint_briefed` is set (testifies, briefly); absent in Bury It late codas (a name on a transfer order Frank reads in the Sacramento paper); absent in Martyr (Dora names him in the column as a source).

## Halston Thorne (the Mayor's brother / conspiracy principal)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_british_sage_posh_wizard` — midlife English posh with a sage, measured cadence; the man who owns the room and has never had to raise his voice to prove it. The "wizard" register reads here as the smug elder-statesman certainty of someone who believes himself the cleverest person in any building. Distinct from Voss's heartfelt municipal posh (warm, apologetic) and from Salzano's dark seductive deep (menace) — Thorne is the register of a person who learned manners as a weapon.
**Body:** a signet ring on the little finger of the left hand he taps once on a table to end a sentence; a tan that has nothing to do with the season — he winters somewhere the city pays for.
**Voice on the page:** Sentences finished. Vowels drawn. Calls Frank "Mr. Cady" the way one calls a tradesman by surname. Quotes nobody, never raises an idiom, never swears. When he is most dangerous he is most polite. Off-page until the Expose coda — only ever named, written about, or seen briefly through a window in Act 2's records-room scene as "a man with a tan in a suit too good for the building."
**Contradiction:** Has never personally killed anyone and is the reason Ray Doss and Vera Doss are dead. Believes he is the civic-minded one in his family — the Mayor is the politician; Thorne is the man who *gets things done* — and the things he gets done are an accounting of corpses he has never had to look at.
**Wants:** the highway through Aldwick on the federal calendar by spring, his brother re-elected, a board seat at a New York bank by Christmas.
**Fears:** that one of the men he has paid is keeping a notebook of his own. (He is correct. Ray Doss kept it.)
**First seen:** Named in the ledger in Act 2 Beat 5 (`ledger_read_then_decide`); named by Kellner in Act 2 Beat 7 (`kellner_topic_name`); glimpsed off-page in Act 2 Beat 4 records-room scene as "a man with a tan in a suit too good for the building." First on-screen presence is in Act 3 coda (`ending_expose`) as the man arrested before his lawyer arrives.
**Recurs in:** Act 3 codas — by name in Expose, by absence in Bury It late (the diner-counter paper names his promotion), by Dora's voiceover in Martyr.
