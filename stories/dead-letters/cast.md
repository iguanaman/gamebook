## Vera Doss (the client)
**Voice:** `female_adult_american_grounded_professional` — her composure is load-bearing; grounded professional fits a woman who has decided not to fall apart in a detective's office
**Body:** keeps her hands folded in her lap like she's practised it; dark coat one size too large, probably Ray's
**Voice on the page:** precise and economical — short sentences, no wandering. When she's frightened she gets quieter, not louder. Never asks Frank to promise something he can't deliver. The one time she cries, she apologises for it.
**Contradiction:** She is the most competent person Frank meets in Act 1. She's already documented the licence plate of the man following her before Frank asks. She treats herself as a supporting character in her own story.
**Wants:** to find Ray; or, failing that, to understand what happened to him and why it mattered
**Fears:** that Ray got himself killed over something that turned out not to matter enough
**First seen:** `office_before`
**Recurs in:** `office_intake_warm`, `office_intake_clipped`, `office_accepts_case`, `vera_call_check`, `vera_describe_follower`, `vera_go_immediately`, `vera_wait_morning`

## Peltz (coworker, nervous witness)
**Voice:** `male_young_american_weak` — the voice mirrors his fundamental problem: he knows what he saw, he lacks the nerve to say it
**Body:** always touches his collar before he speaks; blinks too fast when cornered
**Voice on the page:** sentence fragments and subject changes. He answers questions at an angle — he'll tell you the shape of a thing without the name of it. Filler words: "I mean," "look," "the thing is." When scared, he stops finishing sentences.
**Contradiction:** He is not a coward by nature. He watched Ray take those files home and said nothing because he has children. He knows the difference between the original signature and the forged one — he noticed before Frank asked. A careful man who chose not to be brave.
**Wants:** to not be the person who made the wrong call; to believe staying quiet was the right call
**Fears:** that his silence made him complicit in what happened to Ray
**First seen:** `legwork_survey_dept`
**Recurs in:** `peltz_warm`, `peltz_pressed`, `peltz_watched`, `peltz_files_hint`, `peltz_aldwick_name`, `running_find_peltz`, `peltz_act3_silent`, `peltz_act3_direct`, `peltz_act3_specific`, `peltz_testify_ask`

## Maisie (counter girl, lunch counter)
**Voice:** `female_young_american_sweet_youthful_clear` — a diner counter voice; she says difficult things cheerfully because she hasn't learned yet that some observations carry weight
**Body:** wipes the counter when she's thinking; remembers regulars by their order, not their name
**Voice on the page:** forthcoming without realising she's being forthcoming. Tells stories in the wrong order — starts with what she found interesting, backtracks to the necessary context. No guile at all.
**Contradiction:** She's an excellent witness — observant, specific, emotionally accurate — but she'd never think of herself that way
**Wants:** a quiet shift; to be useful when someone needs it
**Fears:** nothing, yet; the story doesn't get to her
**First seen:** `legwork_lunch_counter`
**Recurs in:** `lunch_counter_company`, `lunch_counter_description`, `lunch_counter_mood`, `lunch_counter_last_day`

## Hector (Aldwick elder, neighbourhood watcher)
**Voice:** `male_elder_american_wise_approachable` — a man who has watched his neighbourhood long enough to read a stranger's angle before the stranger finishes their first sentence
**Body:** sits on a stoop like it's a chair he's earned; doesn't stand up for people he doesn't trust yet
**Voice on the page:** measured and indirect. He answers the question behind the question. Uses "that's what they say" for official versions and plain declaratives for what he actually believes. Slow-spoken — not dim, deliberate.
**Contradiction:** He's a source of warmth and community, but his warmth is conditional. Frank has to earn it. To a stranger he's a closed door. To a neighbour he's the person who keeps the unofficial history.
**Wants:** to protect what's left of the neighbourhood
**Fears:** that he's already too late
**First seen:** `aldwick_elder_approach`
**Recurs in:** `aldwick_hector_direct`, `aldwick_hector_oblique`, `aldwick_hector_badge`, `aldwick_hector_detail` (background texture in `aldwick_hub` Act 2)

## Marta Gaines (Aldwick resident, documentarian)
**Voice:** `female_midlife_african-american_casual_opinionated` — a woman who has been right about something for a year and is tired of being told she isn't; the casual register holds the opinion without raising the volume
**Body:** carries a notebook she doesn't show immediately; stands in doorways rather than inviting you in until she trusts you
**Voice on the page:** factual and specific — she talks in parcel numbers and dates when she trusts you. When she doesn't, she talks in generalities that still somehow contain all the relevant information. Never raises her voice. Never exaggerates. The restraint is its own statement.
**Contradiction:** She is the most meticulous investigator in the story — more systematic than Frank. She's been building the case Ray was building, independently, in a notebook under her mattress. She has never thought to call it investigation.
**Wants:** for someone with the standing she lacks to do something with what she's found
**Fears:** that talking to Frank will get her killed the way it got Ray killed
**First seen:** `aldwick_marta_intro` (Act 1) / `marta_act2_approach` (Act 2)
**Recurs in:** `aldwick_marta_ray`, `aldwick_marta_lead`, `aldwick_marta_wait`, `aldwick_marta_clue`, `aldwick_marta_names`, `marta_act2_full`, `marta_act2_partial`

## Walt Kellner (lieutenant, betrayal pivot)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_american_rich_smooth_rugged` — the warmth reads as genuine until you notice how precisely calibrated it is; smooth and rugged fits a man who has learned to sound like a friend
**Body:** orders coffee and doesn't drink it; hands stay visible on the table — the discipline of a cop who knows what hands in pockets mean
**Voice on the page:** warm, precise, builds rapport with specific memories. He says "Frank" at the start and end of sentences. Uses the past tense fondly, the present tense carefully. When he wants you to do something he presents it as the only reasonable option. He never asks; he clarifies.
**Contradiction:** He is genuinely fond of Frank. The betrayal is not casual. He made a calculation about Frank's value against his own position and Frank came up short — but it costs him something. The warmth is real; so is what he does with it.
**Wants:** to keep his position, his arrangement with Dowd's operation, and if possible his conscience intact; all three at once if the math works out
**Fears:** being asked directly to confirm what he did; he will do anything to avoid the moment of explicit statement
**First seen:** `kellner_first_contact` (by phone, Act 2) / `kellner_arrive` (in person, Act 2)
**Recurs in:** `kellner_hub`, `kellner_frank_past`, `kellner_aldwick`, `kellner_politics`, `kellner_drop_advice`, `kellner_confide`, `kellner_pushout`, `kellner_pushout_confrontation`, `act3_open`, `kellner_meeting_arrival`, `kellner_meeting_cautious`, `kellner_meeting_knowing`, `kellner_meeting_surface`, `kellner_meeting_read`, `kellner_meeting_direct`, `kellner_confront_past`, `kellner_confrontation_open`, `kellner_betrayal_escape`

## Silas Craddock (Aldwick elder, former building inspector)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_american_dark_tough` — he was a man with authority once and the weight of that is still in his voice; the quiet anger of someone who tried the official route and got no reply
**Body:** a copy of the condemnation order in an envelope inside his jacket — he's been carrying it for six months; hands that look like they've held tools
**Voice on the page:** plain and declarative. No hedge words. He says what he saw and what he concluded from it and does not separate the two — in his mind the gap between observation and inference is very small. Occasionally repeats a phrase for emphasis without varying it, the way a man does when he wants to make sure it lands.
**Contradiction:** He sent a letter to his alderman six months ago and got no reply. He's been furious about it ever since. But he's also still here, still on the stoop, still willing to talk to a stranger asking questions — because he can't stop hoping that someone official-adjacent will do something.
**Wants:** for the condemnation to be reversed; failing that, for someone to be held accountable
**Fears:** that he's been made to look like a fool for believing in the process
**First seen:** `craddock_approach`
**Recurs in:** `craddock_hub`, `craddock_inspection`, `craddock_authorisation`, `craddock_others`

## June Holt (Aldwick resident, paper trail keeper)
**Voice:** `female_adult_american_droll_wry_dry` — two years of city men at her door has put a particular finish on her. She's not hostile; she's exact. The dryness is exhaustion worn smooth.
**Body:** opens the door a hand's width before she opens it all the way; keeps a stack of folders on the kitchen table — she doesn't hide them, but she doesn't offer them either
**Voice on the page:** economical and borderline sardonic. She explains the same thing to everyone because everyone is new to it. She's told the story enough times to have edited it to its minimum. The one question she asks Frank — "Is the man who did this going to answer for it?" — she asks once, accepts a non-answer, and drops it.
**Contradiction:** She stayed in her house when three neighbours took the relocation offer and left. Her lease pre-dates the survey. She knows she's protected — legally, barely — but she's treating it as principle rather than self-interest. She'd rather lose the case than take the money.
**Wants:** accountability; and if not accountability, then at least a record that what happened here happened
**Fears:** that she'll be the last one left and still nothing will change
**First seen:** `june_approach`
**Recurs in:** `june_hub`, `june_letter`, `june_contractor`, `june_neighbours`, `running_warn_june`, `running_warned_june_full`, `running_warned_june_spare`

## Dora Reyes (Tribune journalist, package recipient)
**Voice:** `female_adult_american_crisp_direct_clear` — a journalist who has learned to ask one question at a time and wait for the full answer; the crispness is professional, not cold
**Body:** picks up on the third ring and doesn't say hello first — she says the name of the paper; reads without looking up
**Voice on the page:** sparse and precise. Questions are short. Statements are shorter. She doesn't editorialize while Frank is talking. When she says "Are you sure?" at the end of `ending_expose` it's the only moment of register she shows — not doubt, verification.
**Contradiction:** She's been waiting for a call like this, which means she already suspected this story existed. She chose not to chase it without evidence. That restraint is either professionalism or its own kind of courage — the patience to wait for proof instead of running what she suspects.
**Wants:** the story, handled correctly
**Fears:** running it wrong and giving the city machine grounds to discredit it
**First seen:** named in `beat5_depart` and `mail_package` (Act 2); by phone in `running_contact_dora` (Act 3); in person in `ending_expose`
**Recurs in:** `ending_expose`

## Surveying Supervisor (unnamed antagonist-adjacent bureaucrat)
**Voice:** `male_midlife_english_posh` — an officious department head whose authority is borrowed from the men above him; the posh register communicates rank without intelligence; closest available fit — no distinctly officious American midlife voice in library; note this gap if a `male_midlife_american_bureaucratic` voice is added
**Body:** walks through a room without looking at it; has a specific way of not acknowledging Peltz that communicates everything
**Voice on the page:** institutional passive voice when forced to speak. Gives no information directly. Every sentence implies someone above him has made a decision that he is merely conveying.
**Contradiction:** He's not the architect. He's the implementation layer — the man who signs things other men decided. He may not fully understand what he's signing. That doesn't make him innocent.
**Wants:** to continue to be useful to the right people
**Fears:** being in the room when the reckoning happens
**First seen:** `legwork_survey_dept` (background); `supervisor_follow`
**Recurs in:** referenced in `records_who_checked` (checked out the Aldwick files); his signature appears on the forged surveys in Act 2

**Fears:** being connected to whatever Frank is working on
