## Doc Reyes (vault medic, quiet dissident)

**Voice:** `female_adult_american_cool` ŌĆö measured, dry, never raises pitch. The "cool" register fits a clinician who has watched the radiation numbers climb for months and has stopped performing surprise.
**Body:** silver streak above one ear; right hand always wrapped around a coffee mug she rarely drinks from. A row of pens in her coat pocket, one of them a real pre-war ballpoint she will not let anyone touch.
**Voice on the page:** clinical nouns ("presentations", "exposure threshold", "staffing"). Treats horrible things as case notes. Avoids the word "Cole" ŌĆö refers to him as "the overseer" or "administration." Will not joke directly but allows a long pause where a joke would go.
**Contradiction:** the most professional person in the vault is also the most quietly insubordinate. Cole told her to stop documenting the radiation cases; she kept documenting in a private notebook anyway.
**Wants:** the truth recorded somewhere it cannot be erased ŌĆö even if she dies, the notebook survives.
**Fears:** that her own caution killed people she could have warned; that she will have to choose between her oath and her life.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 3 (`medical_office`, `talk_doc_reyes`).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 Atrium hub return visits, Beat 8 recruit (`recruit_doc`), Act 2 as companion across the entire act if Exit 3 / 5 with notebook (`open_with_doc`, `doc_clinic`, `talk_doc_diner`, `consult_doc`, `doc_finds_out`, ending epilogues).

**Voice on the page:** technical and profane. Says "the thing" instead of naming components when she's angry. **Wants:** for someone ŌĆö anyone ŌĆö to make the call to open the door so she can stop being the only thing between 200 people and a meltdown.
**Fears:** that she is more attached to the reactor than to the people; that if the door opens she'll go back to the panel anyway.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 3 (`reactor_deck`, `talk_yuna`).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 stabilise scene (`stabilise_reactor`), Atrium hub returns, Beat 8 recruit (`recruit_yuna`), Act 2 as companion across the entire act if Exit 4 / 5 (`open_with_yuna`, `yuna_radio`, `talk_yuna_diner`, ending epilogues).

## Bregman (overseer's deputy, "administrative liaison")

**Voice:** `male_adult_british_calm_friendly` ŌĆö calm, friendly, never strained; the friendly tag is what makes him unsettling, because the friendliness never wavers regardless of what he is being asked to do. Distinct from Cole's posh-midlife-English overseer voice ŌĆö Bregman is the institutional functionary at one rank lower, softer, no flourishes.
**Body:** tall, shaved head, hands folded in front of him in a stance the training manual no longer teaches. Wears the duty uniform pressed to a crease that suggests he irons it himself.
**Voice on the page:** complete sentences delivered evenly, no hesitation. Distinguishes "courtesy" from "instruction" out loud. Quotes Cole's phrasing back faithfully ŌĆö "the Warden has indicated", "administrative review", "scheduled audit". Never warm, never cold. He says "Officer" the way he would say a file number.
**Contradiction:** kind in small ways ŌĆö his hands on a wounded officer's shoulder are professional, not unkind ŌĆö while serving the man whose orders cause the wounds. He is not cruel and not loyal in any heartfelt sense; he is loyal because loyalty is the shape of the job and the job is the shape of him.
**Wants:** to discharge each instruction precisely, in order, and go home to whatever Bregman has at home.
**Fears:** ambiguity in the chain of command; being asked to improvise.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 1 atrium hover-loop / Beat 5 broadcast-failure (`broadcast_failed_scene`), Beat 6 (`overseer_corridor`).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 Cole-confrontation outcomes (`cole_force_fail`, `cole_force_retreat`, `cole_force_kill`, `cole_blackmail_success`, `cole_join`), broadcast aftermath. Spoken lines so far in `broadcast_failed_scene` and `overseer_corridor`; silent presence elsewhere.

## The Loiterer (atrium fixture, Promenade cynic)

**Voice:** `male_adult_american_casual_relaxed_light` ŌĆö relaxed, light, the casual register of a man who long ago stopped expecting his words to land anywhere important. Not the same family as Brask's witty-casual: the loiterer is softer, slower, more bus-stop than boardroom.
**Body:** mid-fifties, vault blues gone shiny at the elbows. Props the small of his back against the dry fountain. A thermos nobody has ever seen him refill, lifted in small ironic salutes. Taps the side of his nose when conveying gossip.
**Voice on the page:** conversational fragments, repetition for rhythm ("Word from sub-five... Word from the Promenade... Word from the speaker..."). Calls the player "Officer" with affectionate sarcasm, the way you'd address a cousin you owe money to. Mocks Vault-Tec's standing phrases ("atmospheric optimisation") by repeating them in their own register. Drops "Level Three infraction" as a joke about the regime that polices him.
**Contradiction:** the most cynical voice in the atrium is also the one most committed to staying in it ŌĆö he watches the Promenade every shift and has never tried to do anything about what he sees. Awareness is, for him, a substitute for action.
**Wants:** company for the cynicism; to be heard once, by someone who matters, before the lights go out.
**Fears:** that the joke will turn out to have been the point; that his thermos is the only thing keeping him in place.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 2 (`atrium_loiterer`).
**Recurs in:** referenced from `atrium_first`, `atrium_hub`, `broadcast_booth`. Single speaking scene; cameo.

## Warden Aldric Cole (Vault 34 overseer, primary antagonist)

**Voice:** `male_midlife_american_deep_malevolent_ancient_slow` ŌĆö bureaucratic, well-spoken, slightly weary. The posh midlife English register fits the brief's "memo from the overseer" register exactly: institutional language used for atrocity, performed with the calm of a man who genuinely believes he is the adult in the room.
**Body:** Vault-Tec dress uniform always immaculate, but his collar is loose and the skin underneath is grey. A wedding ring on a chain rather than a finger ŌĆö he removed it months ago and won't say why. Sweats more than the air handlers should permit.
**Voice on the page:** complete sentences, subordinate clauses, the passive voice for anything that might be his fault ("a decision was taken", "the report was retained"). Calls residents "the population." Never raises his voice ŌĆö when he is most angry, he goes quieter and more polite. Avoids the words "die", "death", "meltdown" ŌĆö substitutes "attrition", "outcome", "thermal event".
**Contradiction:** genuinely believes he is being merciful. Letting the vault die quietly, in his frame, is kinder than admitting the experiment failed and watching the population tear itself apart in the surface unknown. He is the villain who is right about something ŌĆö the surface really is hostile, and some of his people really will die out there.
**Wants:** to preserve the institution that gives his life meaning ŌĆö the vault, the chain of command, the memos. Survival of the people is secondary to survival of the order.
**Fears:** that he has known for months and could have acted; that history will not record his restraint as wisdom; that he is, in the end, simply a coward in a clean uniform.
**First seen:** Act 1, Beat 6 (`overseer_corridor`, `cole_office`, `cole_confront_force` / `_blackmail` / `cole_join`).
**Recurs in:** Act 1 broadcast aftermath flavour, Act 1 ending preview if `cole_join` (`ending_loyalist_preview`). Act 2 voice on the salvaged radio (`road_cole_voice`, `listen_to_radio`) if `blackmailed_cole` and not `killed_cole`. Act 2 in person at the Diner during `loyalist_remnant_attack` if `confirmed_loyalists`. Final spare/kill choice in Beat 8 endings if present.

## Hatch (Salvager liaison, Act 2 broker)

**Voice:** `female_adult_south-african_bossy_tough` ŌĆö assertive, direct, used to running rooms. The tough register fits a Salvager liaison who handles raider negotiations and water-line politics; the bossy edge keeps her from reading as a softer Doc Reyes echo. The South African accent is the marker that distinguishes her unambiguously from Reyes when they share a scene.
**Body:** a clean patched coat in a wasteland of dirty ones ŌĆö she takes care of her clothes deliberately, as branding. Scarf wound against the dust, pulled down to talk. A small leather notebook she writes in left-handed.
**Voice on the page:** present tense, short clauses, names things by function ("water", "the run", "the message"). Uses Salvager argot when she relaxes ("east hold", "clean line"). Avoids promises ŌĆö says "we can" rather than "we will." Will not flatter the player and will not be flattered.
**Contradiction:** offers help that looks like generosity and is actually recruitment. She is genuinely well-meaning *and* she is running an operation. Both are true at once and she does not see them as contradictions.
**Wants:** to extend the Salvagers' clean-water network east, which means turning every encounter ŌĆö including this one ŌĆö into either an ally, a neutral, or a known threat. No middle ground.
**Fears:** that the Salvagers are slowly becoming what they fled from; that she will recognise the moment too late.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 4b (`salvager_emissary_arrives`).
**Recurs in:** Act 2, Beat 5a (`salvager_outpost`, `talk_hatch`, `hatch_offer_message`, `hatch_offer_medical`), Beat 5c (`message_run_handoff`, `message_run_returned`), Beat 7 (`salvager_rescue`), Open Door / Vault Reborn (Salvager-assisted variant) ending epilogues.

## Brask (Iron Sons raider boss, ex-Salvager)

**Voice:** `male_adult_american_witty_casual` ŌĆö articulate, ironic, lower-register weight than the "casual" tag suggests; the witty register lets the brief's "unexpectedly literate" land. He talks like someone who used to be in meetings and now is not.
**Body:** heavyset, broad through the shoulders, moves slowly because he can. A burn-scar across the back of his neck (from his Salvager days; he won't explain it). Wears a Salvager-cut coat he has not bothered to re-dye.
**Voice on the page:** complete sentences, dry asides, technical vocabulary borrowed wholesale from the Salvagers ("clean line", "east hold", "tribute structure"). Names the player early in the conversation and uses the name often. Avoids any sentimental register ŌĆö when he means something seriously, he gets *less* expressive, not more. Will quote Salvager doctrine and then mock it in the same breath.
**Contradiction:** runs a raider clan with the vocabulary, organisational habits, and quiet decency of the faction he left. He is not a brute ŌĆö he is a Salvager who decided the Salvagers were too slow, and he hates that this makes him a worse person and is doing it anyway.
**Wants:** territory and supply lines east of Bakersfield; to prove the Salvager method works faster when you stop pretending you're above coercion.
**Fears:** that Hatch is right about him; that the day will come when his crew realises he still talks like a Salvager and decides he isn't one of them after all.
**First seen:** Act 2, Beat 5a ŌĆö first *named* in Hatch dialogue (`hatch_topic_raiders`). First *met in person* in Act 2, Beat 5b (`raider_camp`, `raider_pact_scene`).
**Recurs in:** Beat 5c if `road_brask_recognises_you` fires, Beat 7 (`raider_pact_visit` if pact honoured; off-screen presence behind the raid otherwise), Warden's Ghost ending epilogue as the player's new peer.
